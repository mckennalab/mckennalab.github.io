Every cell can trace its ancestry back through successive divisions to the beginnings of life. We are interested in this pattern of ancestry within an organism: how one cell becomes many in model systems such as mice or in diseases such as cancer.

About the lab

Our group intersects computational biology, single-cell sequencing, and CRISPR/Cas9 genome engineering to map cell fate within development and cancer.

We’re looking for people who share these interests and are looking for post-docs or research scientists to come join us at the Geisel School of Medicine at Dartmouth. We also accept grad student rotations from the Molecular and Cellular Biology Program and the Graduate Program in Quantitative Biomedical Sciences. Students from other programs are welcome to rotate according to the rules of their program.
